Projects

In community college, I was lucky to participate in NCAS, NASA’s Community College Aerospace Scholars program. To participate, I had to apply with my GPA, a recommendation from a superior, and an essay outlining why I should be awarded this opportunity. I was accepted, and during the program, I was put in a team of 7 where we had to build a replica of a potential future Mars’ rover, and program it to complete an obstacle course. My team won the competition between 3 other groups. During this project, I learned how to work in a STEM work environment, network with NASA and Google employees, and learned about the Space and Aerospace industry. I highly recommend participating in the project for community college students, more information can be found here!
Although not traditionally seen as a project, a person’s work no doubt allows them to learn new skills, practice pre existing stills, gain experience, and network. As such, I have put my work as a barista at Erewhon as a project of mine. Here, I have learned a number of different skills, including staying calm in a high-demand, fast-place environment, and working in a very diverse environment. Adding, I have networked with both higher management and customers who work in the industry I wish to have a career in. Erewhon has given me the opportunity to expand my skill set, and network with potential industry hirers. You can visit the Erewhon website here: Erewhon
